# Module 14: Theory & Foundations - Convergence, Step-Size, and Information Geometry

Time to peek under the hood. Why does CMA-ES work so well? What guarantees convergence? How fast can ES converge in theory? This module connects the intuition from earlier modules to rigorous mathematics - step-size adaptation theory, convergence rate analysis, and the beautiful information-geometric perspective that unifies NES, CMA-ES, and natural gradients.

## Learning Objectives

1. Understand linear convergence on the sphere function and what convergence rate means for ES.
2. Derive the cumulative step-size adaptation (CSA) mechanism and why it controls step size.
3. Understand the information-geometric foundation: Fisher information matrix, natural gradient, and their role in CMA-ES and NES.
4. Study the lower bounds on convergence rates for comparison-based algorithms.
5. Connect ES theory to broader optimization theory (no free lunch, black-box complexity).
