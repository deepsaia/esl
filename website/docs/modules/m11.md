# Module 11: Quality Diversity — MAP-Elites and Illumination Algorithms

Sometimes you don't want just the single best solution — you want a diverse archive of high-performing solutions that cover different behavioral niches. Quality-Diversity algorithms like MAP-Elites produce illuminating maps of what's possible, not just what's optimal. The results are often surprising and beautiful.

## Learning Objectives

1. Understand the QD paradigm: optimizing for both quality and diversity simultaneously.
2. Implement MAP-Elites from scratch on a simple domain.
3. Understand novelty search and surprise search as alternatives to objective-driven optimization.
4. Use `qdax` for GPU-accelerated QD on a locomotion task.
5. Analyze and visualize behavior-performance archives.
